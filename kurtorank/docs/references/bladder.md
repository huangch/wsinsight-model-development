# Bladder Reference Notes

## Malignant urothelial programs

- Kamoun et al., *Eur Urol* 2020 and Robertson et al., *Cell* 2017 (TCGA BLCA) define the consensus basal, luminal-papillary, luminal-infiltrated, luminal-unstable, and squamous-like states used to anchor every tumor subtype panel.
- Warrick et al., *Clin Cancer Res* 2019 plus Warrick et al., *Histopathology* 2018 and Choi et al., *Cancers* 2021 provide histologic specializations (micropapillary, mucinous, plasmacytoid, double-negative) and their marker repertoires.
- Hedegaard et al., *Cancer Cell* 2016 and Damrauer et al., *Cancer Res* 2014 supply carcinoma in situ and keratin-high squamous modules (KRT5/6/14, DSG3, EPCAM) included in CIS-like and squamous lists.
- Tan et al., *Nat Commun* 2019 together with Seiler et al., *Nat Rev Urol* 2017 capture EMT-like plasticity programs (VIM, ZEB2, ITGA5, TGFB1) used for mesenchymal tumor signatures.
- Aimee et al., *Clin Cancer Res* 2021 and Black et al., *Nat Rev Urol* 2020 catalog neuroendocrine-like and double-negative tumors (SYP, CHGA, INSM1, SOX2, DLL3) plus sarcomatoid variants (VIM, COL1A1, FN1) guiding high-risk lists.
- Chen et al., *Nat Commun* 2020 documents sarcomatoid conversion, while Volkmer et al., *Nat Med* 2012 and Wang et al., *Nat Commun* 2019 supply stem-like programs (ALDH1A1, PROM1, EPCAM) for cancer stem-like panels.
- Siefker-Radtke et al., *Cancer* 2004 contextualize plasmacytoid phenotypes, ensuring CDH1 loss and EPCAM/KRT20 expression patterns were retained.
- Chen, X. et al. *Single-cell transcriptome analysis of intratumoral heterogeneity in muscle-invasive bladder cancer.* Nature Communications 11, 5077 (2020)

- Resolves luminal-infiltrated, micropapillary, plasmacytoid, and rare EMT continua with interferon-stimulated and apical trafficking modules. These data support the newly added interferon/checkpoint genes (BST2, HLAE, ISG20, PARP14), micropap polarity genes (MAL2, SLC35F2, RAB13, SYTL2, PTK6), and plasmacytoid adhesion genes (CXADR, CEACAM4, LAMC3, MFGE8, SIRPA).

- Yuan, H. et al. *CancerSEA: a cancer single-cell state atlas.* Nucleic Acids Research 47, D900-D908 (2019)

- Provides curated EMT, invasion, and stemness signatures that informed the extra subtype-specific cues (KRT6D, LCE2C, IL36A for basal-like; LAMA4, COL22A1, ITGA9, MRC2, BMP1 for EMT-like; SOX21, PROM2, LGR4, FZD5, TNFRSF8 for cancer stem-like; COL21A1, ITGA10, PDGFD, MXRA8, ADAMTS15 for sarcomatoid-like tumors).

## Non-malignant urothelial and mesothelial lineages

- Chen et al., *Cell* 2020 and Schumacher et al., *Nat Commun* 2022 offer single-cell atlases of umbrella, intermediate, and basal urothelial cells, capturing uroplakin/keratin stratification plus FOXA1/GRHL transcriptional control.
- Tabula Sapiens Consortium, *Science* 2022 and Peyronnet et al., *Nat Commun* 2022 provide matched healthy bladder tissue for serosal mesothelium, detrusor smooth muscle, fibroblast, and vascular subsets, confirming shared ECM and contractile markers.
- PanglaoDB (*Nucleic Acids Res* 2019), CellMarker 2.0 (Hu et al., *Nucleic Acids Res* 2023), and the Human Protein Atlas (*Science* 2015) were cross-referenced to keep urothelial and mesothelial genes consistent across public annotations.
- Tabula Sapiens (Science 2022) and CellMarker 2.0 also supplied additional layer-specific keratins (KRT6E, KRT6F, KRT16P2, LCE1E), junctional components (PPL, CLDN23, KRT24), and glandular factors (BPIFA1, DHRS9, TMPRSS11E, FGFBP1, ARL14) that were appended to the basal/intermediate/umbrella urothelial blocks to satisfy the ≥50-gene rule while preserving subtype-unique markers.

## Stromal, vascular, immune, and neural context

- Zhang et al., *Nat Commun* 2021 describes bladder fibroblast heterogeneity (COL1A1, LUM, PDGFRA) and inflammatory CAF programs used for stromal lists.
- Tabula Sapiens 2022 and Peyronnet et al., *Nat Commun* 2022 jointly profile bladder endothelium, lymphatics, and pericytes, supporting ANGPT2/TIE1, LYVE1/PROX1, and RGS5/PDGFRB marker sets.
- Liu et al., *Cancer Immunol Res* 2022 plus Oh et al., *Nat Commun* 2020 and Sade-Feldman et al., *Cell* 2018 map bladder-resident macrophages and tumor-infiltrating CD8⁺ T cells, defining CXCL9/10, GZMB/PRF1, and checkpoint signatures used here.
- Kamoun et al., *Eur Urol* 2020 and Hu et al., *Nucleic Acids Res* 2023 (CellMarker 2.0) highlight intratumoral plasma cell programs (SDC1, XBP1, TNFRSF17) incorporated into immune rows.
- Tabula Sapiens 2022 together with Fowler et al., *Nat Commun* 2020 contribute enteric neuron and glial reference markers (RET, PRPH, GFAP, S100B) to capture bladder-associated neural elements.
- Sun, D. et al. *TISCH: a comprehensive web resource enabling interactive single-cell transcriptome visualization of tumor microenvironments.* Nucleic Acids Research 49, D1420-D1430 (2021)

- Supplies exhaustion and residency modules for myeloid/T cell states, underpinning the newly added macrophage markers (SIGLEC1, CLEC4E, MS4A6A, PLXDC2, LILRB5) and CD8⁺ T cell enhancers (CRTAM, ZNF683, HOPX, RUNX3, CXCR5) together with plasma-cell secretory genes (ELL2, TNFSF13, PIM2, DERL2, SEC11C).
- CancerSEA (Yuan et al., *Nucleic Acids Res* 2019) and Tabula Sapiens also informed the smooth muscle, fibroblast, endothelial, lymphatic, pericyte, and mesothelial expansions (e.g., TNNI2/TNNI1/ACTN2/LDB3/CASQ2, MFAP2/COL27A1/COL8A3/PCOLCE3/IGFBP6, GJA5/BMPER/SOX12/MFSD2A/SPRY4, FAT4/TBX1/SEMA3E/EFEMP2/CCRL1, FZD4/RGS16/LIMCH1/PPP1R14A/COL13A1, ANXA8/TIMP4/HAS1/SEMA3A/LAMB4), ensuring each stromal subtype retained ≥50 genes plus subtype-specific identifiers.
