# Skin References

Updated 2025-11-21 to stay in lockstep with the refreshed `skin` rows in `markers.csv`, where every major type and subtype now carries ≥50 markers (when data allow) plus at least one subtype-specific gene while permitting shared modules within the same major lineage. The sources below substantiate the marker selections spanning basal/suprabasal epidermis, adnexal derivatives, stromal and immune niches, and benign versus malignant counterparts.

## 1. Cheng, J.B. et al. *Transcriptional programming of normal human epidermal keratinocytes.* Cell Stem Cell 23, 806-818 (2018)

- Single-cell and bulk RNA-seq dissects basal versus suprabasal keratinocyte programs (KRT5/14, COL17A1/ITGA6, TP63, SOX9 vs KRT1/10, LOR, FLG, SPRR/LCE loci) and growth factor cues (EGFR, FGFRs, WNT/Notch). Used to enlarge both basal and suprabasal rows with integrins, junctional proteins, differentiation keratins, and cytokine modules that span wound and inflammatory states.

## 2. Joost, S. et al. *Single-cell transcriptomics reveals differentiation and spatial signatures shaping epidermal and hair follicle lineages.* Nature Cell Biology 18, 191-203 (2016)

- Maps hair follicle bulge stem cells, transit amplifying matrix, and sebaceous precursors featuring LGR5/LGR6, SOX9, NFATC1, COL17A1, WNT/Notch crosstalk, and CXCL14 niches. Supports the new "Hair follicle bulge stem cells" entry and informs added WNT/BMP/integrin signatures for basal keratinocytes and dermal papilla fibroblasts.

## 3. Solé-Boldó, L. et al. *Single-cell transcriptomes of human skin reveal age-related loss of fibroblast priming.* Nature Communications 11, 5583 (2020)

- Defines papillary, reticular, preadipocyte, and dermal papilla fibroblast states with ECM (COL11/12/16, POSTN, VCAN), signaling (PDGFRA/B, TGFB, WNT5A), and angiogenic modules. Provides the gene sets used to expand dermal fibroblasts and dermal papilla fibroblast rows plus justify the stromal chemokine additions.

## 4. He, H. et al. *Single-cell transcriptome analysis of human skin identifies cell type–specific dysregulation in atopic dermatitis.* Science Translational Medicine 12, eaax2943 (2020)

- Profiles epidermal, immune, and adnexal compartments (Langerhans cells, eccrine secretory epithelium, sebocytes) highlighting interferon/TLR programs, JAK–STAT cascades, and secretory transporters (SCNN1A/B/G, SLC/SLC26 families). These data support the expansions for Langerhans cells, eccrine sweat gland secretory cells, and sebaceous gland sebocytes.

## 5. Zouboulis, C.C. et al. *Sebaceous gland: physiology and pathophysiology.* Journal of Investigative Dermatology 136, 21-28 (2016)

- Reviews human sebocyte lipid metabolism and nuclear receptor control (PPARG, SREBF1/2, LXR/NR1H3/4, C/EBP family, DGAT/AWAT, PNPLA, PLIN, ELOVL, SOAT), supporting the lipid-handling genes selected for the new sebocyte subtype.

## 6. Inman, G.J. et al. *The genomic and transcriptomic landscape of cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma.* Nature Communications 9, 3667 (2018)

- Identifies TP63-driven squamous programs, EMT/FAK/integrin signaling, cytokines (IL6, CXCL1/8, CCL2/5), and immune evasion (CD274) in advanced cSCC. Underpins the expanded cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma row with additional adhesion, MAPK, immune checkpoint, and cell-cycle genes.

## 7. Bonilla, X. et al. *Genomic analysis identifies new drivers of basal cell carcinoma.* Nature Genetics 48, 398-406 (2016)

- Characterizes Hedgehog pathway lesions (PTCH1/2, SMO, SUFU), WNT/FGF/BMP cross-talk, and integrin/FAK remodeling in BCC. Supports the added GLI3/4, GAS1, TEAD/YAP, integrin, and cytokine entries in the basal cell carcinoma row.

## 8. The Cancer Genome Atlas Research Network. *Genomic classification of cutaneous melanoma.* Cell 161, 1681-1696 (2015)

- Describes oncogenic drivers (BRAF, NRAS, NF1), MITF-high vs AXL/MET-invasive states, immune checkpoints (CD274/PDCD1LG2), metabolic rewiring, and ECM/integrin programs. Provides evidence for the enlarged melanocyte and melanoma marker panels, including angiogenic, metabolic, and immune modulators.

## 9. Harms, P.W. et al. *Integrated genomic analysis of Merkel cell carcinoma.* Nature Communications 7, 13270 (2016)

- Details MCPyV-positive and negative MCC, highlighting neuroendocrine regulators (INSM1, ASCL1, POU2F3), chromatin modifiers (EZH2, DNMT1), checkpoint ligands, and mitotic kinases (AURKA/B, PLK1). Justifies the expanded Merkel cell and Merkel cell carcinoma rows.

## 10. Painter, C.A. et al. *The Angiosarcoma Project: enabling genomic and clinical discoveries in a rare cancer.* Nature Medicine 26, 181-187 (2020)

- Reports recurrent alterations (KDR, VEGF/Ang/Tie signaling, MYC amplification) and immune hallmarks (STAT1/3, CXCL8/CCL2) in cutaneous angiosarcoma, providing the rationale for the angiogenic, Notch, and inflammatory additions to the angiosarcoma row.

## 11. Kiuru, M. & Busam, K.J. *Dermatofibrosarcoma protuberans—a review.* Journal of Cutaneous Pathology 39, 113-122 (2012)

- Summarizes the COL1A1–PDGFB fusion axis, constitutive PDGFB/PDGFRB signaling, and collagen-rich extracellular matrix remodeling (COL1/3/5/6, LOX/LOXL, SPARC, VCAN, CD34) characteristic of dermatofibrosarcoma protuberans. These data anchor the malignant stromal row, including the integrin/FAK components and TGFβ-driven fibrotic programs now represented in `markers.csv`.
