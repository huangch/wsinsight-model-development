# Prostate References

## 1. The Cancer Genome Atlas Research Network. *The molecular taxonomy of primary prostate cancer.* Cell 163, 1011-1025 (2015)

- Multi-omic analysis of 333 primary tumors defining ERG-fusion luminal tumors, SPOP-mutant luminal, CHD1-deleted, and other lineages with accompanying RNA programs.
- Provides marker support for luminal epithelial/tumor panels (KLK3, NKX3-1, FOXA1, AR, TMPRSS2) and basal-to-EMT continua (KRT5/14, ITGA6/B4, VIM) already present in `markers.csv`.

## 2. Abida, W. et al. *Genomic correlates of clinical outcome in advanced prostate cancer.* Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences 116, 11428-11436 (2019) (SU2C/PCF cohort)

- Characterizes AR-positive, AR-low/AR-indifferent, double-negative, and lineage-plastic CRPC states with recurrent alterations (RB1/TP53/PTEN loss, MYCN/AURKA/SOX2 amplifications, FGFR/RET activation, PI3K signaling).
- Informs the expanded AR-low and lineage-plastic marker lists (YAP1/TEADs, WWTR1, FGFR1/2/3, RET, BRN2/POU3F2, SRRM4, BCL11B, EMT regulators) plus interferon/inflammatory resistant signatures.

## 3. Beltran, H. et al. *Divergent clonal evolution of castration-resistant neuroendocrine prostate cancer.* Nature Medicine 22, 298-305 (2016)

- Defines NEPC transcriptional programs (CHGA/B, SYP, INSM1, ASCL1, DLL3, PEG10, SRRM4, MYCN, AURKA, SOX11) and mixed neuroendocrine/adenocarcinoma hybrids.
- Supports the castration-resistant neuroendocrine-like and NEPC marker expansions (SRRM4, POU3F2, BEX2, SOX11, DLL4, NCAM family genes).

## 4. Karthaus, W.R. et al. *Regenerative potential of prostate luminal cells revealed by single-cell analysis.* Cell 176, 1188-1206 (2019)

- Single-cell and organoid profiling uncover basal, luminal, hillock, club, and tuft/POU2F3⁺ secretory populations plus stromal niches.
- Provides the gene sets for club cells (SCGB3A1/A2, WFDC-family, TACSTD2) and justifies adding the Tuft (POU2F3⁺) secretory cell row with TRPM5, DCLK1, LRMP, IL17RB/IL25, WFDC2/8/11, TAS2Rs, chemokines.

## 5. Chen, S. et al. *Single-cell transcriptomes define lineage plasticity and therapy-resistant prostate cancer states.* Science 373, eabd4216 (2021)

- Profiles metastatic biopsies highlighting lineage-plastic/transitional, AR-low/indifferent, interferon-responsive, and SCL-high TACSTD2⁺ states with transcription-factor rewiring (TEAD/YAP, WWTR1, CTNNB1, STAT3, GBP family, ISG signatures).
- Used to curate the added transcriptional regulators and interferon-stimulated gene expansions (GBP1-5, BST2, HLA-DRA/B1, USP18, OASL, IFI16).

## 6. Tabula Sapiens Consortium. *The Tabula Sapiens: A multiple-organ single-cell transcriptomic atlas of the human body.* Science 376, eabl4896 (2022)

- Provides reference expression for prostate stromal and vascular compartments including fibroblasts, pericytes, lymphatic endothelial cells (FOXC2, SOX17, KLF2/4, GJA4/5, MFSD2A), Schwann cells (PRX, GJB1, ERBB4, LAMA4/B1), and adipocytes.
- Corroborates the stromal/vascular marker augmentations and the inclusion of circulating-like stromal rarities.

## 7. Beltrao-Braga, P.C.B. et al. *Organoid modeling of human prostate development and disease.* Nature Communications 13, 5742 (2022)

- Demonstrates differentiation of basal progenitors into luminal, neuroendocrine, and tuft-like secretory cells within prostate organoids, emphasizing PRDM1/BCL11A programs and chemokine production in tuft states.
- Supports the tuft-cell marker combination (PRDM1, BCL11A, cytokine/chemokine axes, TAS2Rs) and their inclusion as an uncommon epithelial subtype.
