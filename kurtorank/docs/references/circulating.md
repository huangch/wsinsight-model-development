# Circulating Cell References

## 1. Dzierzak, E. & Philipsen, S. *Erythropoiesis: development and differentiation.* Cold Spring Harbor Perspectives in Medicine 3, a011601 (2013)

- Reviews human definitive erythropoiesis with emphasis on globin expression (HBA/HBB/HBG/HBD), heme biosynthesis (ALAS2, FECH, SLC25A37/SLC25A38), cytoskeletal anchoring (SLC4A1, ANK1, EPB41/42, SPTA1/B), and transcriptional regulators (GATA1, KLF1, BCL11A).
- Supports the enriched erythrocyte marker panel used in `markers.csv`, including the added mitochondrial transporters and fetal/adult globins.

## 2. Italiano, J.E. & Battinelli, E.M. *Selective sorting of alpha-granule proteins in megakaryocytes and platelets.* Journal of Thrombosis and Haemostasis 7, 173 (2009)

- Describes platelet biogenesis and the molecular components of platelet signaling (PF4, PF4V1, PPBP, ITGA2B/ITGB3, GP1BA/B, GP9, FERMT2/3) and activation pathways (P2RY12, PLEK, SRC, SYK, STIM1).
- Provides justification for the platelet marker list, particularly the integrin adaptors (FERMT2) and chemokines newly appended.

## 3. Woywodt, A. et al. *Circulating endothelial cells as markers for angiogenesis and vascular damage.* Haematologica 91, 165-172 (2006)

- Defines circulating endothelial cells (CECs) with phenotypic markers (PECAM1, CDH5, KDR, TIE1/2, ROBO4, CLDN5, MCAM, ENG, ESM1) and their role in vascular injury and cancer.
- Underpins the CEC row in `markers.csv`, including shear-responsive transcription factors (ERG, GATA2, HEY1/2) and adhesion molecules.

## 4. Asahara, T. et al. *Isolation of putative progenitor endothelial cells for angiogenesis.* Science 275, 964-967 (1997)

- First description of CD34⁺/CD133⁺/KDR⁺ endothelial progenitor cells (EPCs) mobilized from bone marrow and circulating in peripheral blood.
- Provides the canonical EPC markers (PROM1, KDR, TEK, CXCR4, NOTCH1/4, NRP1/2) included in the circulating block.

## 5. da Silva Meirelles, L. et al. *Circulating mesenchymal stem cells: reality or myth?* Stem Cells and Development 15, 249-257 (2006)

- Summarizes evidence for blood-borne mesenchymal stem/stromal cells expressing CD105, CD73, CD90, PDGFRA/B, ITGA5/ITGB1, THY1, CXCL12, and EMT/osteogenic regulators (SOX9, RUNX2, WNT5A).
- Justifies the circulating MSC marker set and the overlapping pericyte-like signatures (PDGFRB, CSPG4, RGS5, NOTCH3, ADRA1B, PDE5A).

## 6. Reilkoff, R.A., Bucala, R. & Herzog, E.L. *Fibrocytes: emerging effector cells in chronic inflammation.* Nature Reviews Immunology 11, 427-435 (2011)

- Details circulating fibrocytes derived from monocytes, characterized by co-expression of mesenchymal ECM genes (COL1A1/2, COL3A1, COL5A1/2, COL14A1, FN1), leukocyte markers (CD34, PTPRC, CD11b), chemokine receptors (CXCR4, CCR5/7/2), and profibrotic mediators (TGFBR2, SMAD3/4, POSTN, LOX, MMP2/9/14, ADAM12).
- Directly supports the new `Circulating fibrocytes` row and its marker composition.
