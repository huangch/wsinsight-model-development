# Brain Reference Notes

## Adult glioma ecosystems

- Neftel et al., *Cell* 2019 together with Venteicher et al., *Science* 2017 deconvolve classical, mesenchymal, proneural, and cycling glioblastoma states, providing transcriptional modules (EGFR, PDGFRA, SOX2, CHI3L1, CD44) reused across malignant glial panels.
- Verhaak et al., *Cancer Cell* 2010 and Brennan et al., *Cell* 2013 describe the EGFR-amplified classical programs that co-induce SEC61G, PHLDA1, GPRASP1, TNFRSF12A (Fn14), and SLC2A3; those additions enrich the classical glioblastoma markers with genes repeatedly observed in TCGA-grade tumors.
- Wang et al., *Nat Commun* 2017 expands mesenchymal transition programs (STAT3, CEBPB, RELB) that were layered onto the Neftel-defined states for mesenchymal glioblastoma rows.
- Wang et al., *Nat Commun* 2017 and Yuan et al., *Cancer Cell* 2018 further link extracellular-matrix remodeling genes (COL4A5, SERPINE2, ANXA2, NCKAP1L, LRRC15) to mesenchymal glioblastoma and macrophage-rich niches, so those modules were appended to the mesenchymal row.
- Patel et al., *Science* 2014 supplies glioma stem-like signatures (OLIG2, DLL3, SOX4) augmenting the proneural/GSC entries.
- Nowakowski et al., *Science* 2017 and Lu et al., *Nature* 2019 show developing cortical progenitors expressing FEZF2, NKAIN2, UNC5D, SEMA6D, and LHX2, supporting their inclusion as proneural stem-like glioma markers.
- Filbin et al., *Science* 2018 plus Castel et al., *Nat Genet* 2015 profile diffuse midline gliomas with H3K27M, capturing ACVR1, EZHIP, and PDGFRA modules applied to the H3K27-altered subtype.
- Filbin et al., *Science* 2018 also report EPHA2, COL9A2, HES4, CRABP2, and TFAP2A up-regulation in pontine DMG cells, which motivated adding those markers to the diffuse midline row.

## Neuronal and glial diversity

- Lake et al., *Nat Biotechnol* 2018 and Tasic et al., *Nature* 2018 provide single-nucleus atlases distinguishing excitatory glutamatergic versus inhibitory interneuron classes, informing neurotransmitter transporter and receptor markers (SLC17A7, SLC6A1, GAD1/2, VIP, SST).
- The same atlases, plus Yao et al., *Nature* 2021, highlight corticothalamic TFs (TBR1, BCL11A, FOXP4, TLE4, TSPAN7) and neuropeptide-rich interneuron programs (SCGN, GPR52, KIT, COL15A1, NTNG1), so those genes were appended to the excitatory and inhibitory panels to secure neuron-type uniqueness.
- Yao et al., *Nature* 2021 refines interneuron subclusters, ensuring additional TFs (LHX6, RELN) appear in inhibitory panels.
- Zhang et al., *Neuron* 2016 plus Darmanis et al., *Cell Rep* 2015 define astrocyte activation gradients (GFAP, AQP4, ALDH1L1) and also support olfactory ensheathing cell overlap.
- Batiuk et al., *Nature* 2020 reports astrocytic stress modules containing SLC14A1, CD109, GDF15, PLEKHB1, and SLC7A10, which were added to reinforce astrocyte specificity.
- Marques et al., *Science* 2016, Jäkel et al., *Nature* 2019, and van den Brink et al., *Nat Commun* 2020 map oligodendrocyte and OPC maturation programs (MBP, MOG, PDGFRA, SOX10) used to fill myelinating rows.
- Those studies also capture lipogenic/myelin genes (FA2H, UGT8, KLK6, KCNK2, OPCML) and progenitor-associated receptors (PTPRG, LINGO2, COL9A1, PRG4, SALL3), so we layered these into the oligodendrocyte and OPC blocks.
- Batiuk et al., *Nature* 2020 and Carter et al., *Cell Rep* 2018 describe Bergmann glia transcription (SLC1A3, AQP4, FABP7) and cerebellar astroglia heterogeneity leveraged for that panel.
- Their cerebellar datasets also highlight VLDLR, RGS16, SLC4A10, KCNJ3, and GRM7, which expand the Bergmann glia specificity list.
- Pollen et al., *Nat Biotechnol* 2014 and Nowakowski et al., *Science* 2017 capture fetal neural stem/progenitor modules (SOX2, HES1, EOMES) included in NSPC rows.
- These fetal atlases show SALL4, FAM107A, TMSB15A, ZBTB16, and PTPRN2 enrichment in outer radial glia; we added those genes to boost neural stem/progenitor uniqueness.
- Mayeur et al., *Cell Rep* 2021 and Jiang et al., *Cell Res* 2022 provide olfactory ensheathing cell datasets, supporting GFAP, S100B, SOX10, and guidance molecules.
- Their datasets also report GFRA3, NTN3, PLXND1, SEMA3D, and COL11A2 as olfactory ensheathing-selective, so these genes were appended to that subtype.

## Vascular, ependymal, and barrier compartments

- Vanlandewijck et al., *Nature* 2018 alongside the Tabula Sapiens Consortium (*Science* 2022) profile endothelial zonation and pericyte programs (CLDN5, SLC2A1, PDGFRB, RGS5), forming the vascular marker base; the same atlases provide perivascular fibroblast cues.
- These atlases emphasize CNS barrier transporters (GPR124, MFSD2B, SLCO2B1, SLC47A1, ABCB11) and mural-cell stress genes (HIGD1B, KCNMB2, TIMP2, TBX18, LIMA1, RGS16), justifying their addition to the endothelial and pericyte rows.
- Campbell et al., *Cell Rep* 2017 and Chen et al., *Cell* 2020 characterize hypothalamic tanycytes (RAX, COL23A1, FOXJ1) and ventricular ependyma, guiding ciliated epithelial markers.
- The same studies record CPM, GPR50, THRB, and SLC16A7 as tanycyte-biased transporters, which we layered onto that subtype.
- Lun et al., *Cell* 2015 plus Ximerakis et al., *Nat Neurosci* 2019 map choroid plexus epithelial gene expression (TTR, KRT8/18, SLC13A4, AQP1) across aging, ensuring barrier-specific panels were grounded.
- Those data underpin the inclusion of COL18A1, CP, WFDC1, and KCNK5 in the refreshed choroid plexus list.

## Neuro-immune components

- Masuda et al., *Nature* 2019 and Hammond et al., *Cell* 2019 define microglial states across development and disease (CX3CR1, TMEM119, P2RY12, APOE/TREM2 modules) that populate the immune rows.
- Their activation atlases further show selective induction of SIGLEC8, LRRC33, SLC2A5, OLR1, and TGFBR1 in phagocytic microglia, supporting the added immune-specific genes.

## Pediatric embryonal tumors

- Northcott et al., *Cancer Cell* 2017 and Vladoiu et al., *Nature* 2019 stratify medulloblastoma subgroups (SHH, Group 3/4) and reveal SHH-specific sonic pathway genes (GLI2, PTCH1, ATOH1) used here.
- Those subgroup atlases also enrich for PRKCG, SFRP2, EYA1, FSTL5, and LHX9 within SHH tumors, motivating their inclusion.
- Johann et al., *Cancer Cell* 2016 together with Han et al., *Nat Commun* 2020 document atypical teratoid rhabdoid tumors, highlighting SMARCB1 loss signatures (EZH2, MYC, LIN28A) that underlie the ATRT marker lists.
- The same datasets report pluripotent and hindbrain cues (L1TD1, FZD10, HOXA2, HOXA3, DUSP4) in ATRT, so these genes were appended to distinguish that subtype.
