# Liver References

## 1. Aizarani, N. et al. *A human liver cell atlas reveals heterogeneity and epithelial progenitors.* Nature 572, 199-204 (2019)

- Single-cell RNA-seq of fresh human liver dissociates portal-central zonated hepatocytes, cholangiocytes, liver sinusoidal endothelial cells (LSECs), hepatic stellate cells, and Kupffer macrophages.
- Provides zonation-supported hepatocyte panels (CYP2E1, CYP3A4, GLUL, ASS1, HAL, APOA1) and cholangiocyte/progenitor signatures (KRT7/8/18/19, EPCAM, TACSTD2, SOX9, CFTR, ANXA4) already represented in `markers.csv`.

## 2. MacParland, S.A. et al. *Single cell RNA sequencing of human liver reveals distinct intrahepatic macrophage populations.* Nature Communications 9, 4383 (2018)

- Defines Kupffer cells versus monocyte-derived macrophages using TIMD4, CLEC4F, VSIG4, MARCO, SPIC, FOLR2, and lipid-handling programs.
- Supports the dedicated Kupffer row and validates their complement/ scavenger receptor-rich marker set (C1QA/B/C, TREM2, MSR1, LPL) coupled with IL-10 signaling (IL10RA/B) and heme metabolism (HMOX1, SLC40A1).

## 3. Ramachandran, P. et al. *Resolving the fibrotic niche of human liver cirrhosis at single-cell level.* Nature 575, 512-520 (2019)

- Captures perisinusoidal stellate subtypes (quiescent, activated myofibroblasts) expressing RBP1, LRAT, PDGFRB, COL1A1, ACTA2, TAGLN, MFAP4, LOXL2, and chemokine/cytokine ligands.
- Informs the stellate/fibroblast marker lists and highlights interactions with Kupffer and endothelial cells through VCAM1/ICAM1/CCL chemokines already in the table.

## 4. Tabula Sapiens Consortium. *The Tabula Sapiens: A multiple-organ single-cell transcriptomic atlas of the human body.* Science 376, eabl4896 (2022)

- Cross-organ atlas validating liver endothelial heterogeneity (LSEC: PECAM1, ACKR1, STAB1/2, LYVE1, FCN2; macrovascular EC: VWF, F8, KDR) and portal fibroblasts.
- Provides confirmatory evidence for the endothelial, fibroblast, and immune/stromal rows and their zonation-aware marker mixes.

## 5. Peng, H. et al. *Liver-resident NK cells confer adaptive immunity against viral infection.* Nature Immunology 14, 1234-1240 (2013)

- Characterizes hepatic pit (liver-resident) NK cells with CXCR6/CXCR3 chemokine receptors, integrins (ITGA1, ITGB1/2), killer lectin receptors (KLRB1, KLRC1/2, KLRD1, KLRK1), cytotoxic granules (PRF1, GZMB, GZMK, GNLY), and transcription factors (EOMES, TBX21, RUNX3).
- Provides the basis for the new "Hepatic pit NK cells" subtype plus inclusion of SH2D1A/SLAMF receptors and FCGR3A/LILRB inhibitory receptors.

## 6. Gola, A. et al. *Commensal-driven immune zonation of the liver promotes intrahepatic tolerance.* Cell 178, 1198-1215 (2019)

- Describes Kupffer–pit cell cross-talk and zonated immune microenvironments, citing chemokines (CXCL16, CCL5), cytokines (IFNG, TNF), and adhesion molecules (VCAM1, ICAM1) that we include across immune rows.
- Justifies pairing Kupffer cell chemokine receptor sets (CXCR4, CCR2, CCR5) with pit-cell ligand expression to capture tolerogenic versus cytotoxic states.
