# Colorectal References

## 1. The Cancer Genome Atlas Network. *Comprehensive molecular characterization of human colon and rectal cancer.* Nature 487, 330-337 (2012). doi:10.1038/nature11252

- Multi-omic profiling (RNA-seq, copy number, exome, methylation) across 276 TCGA-COAD/READ tumors that resolve stem-like/TA, WNT-high enterocyte-like, goblet-like, and mucinous/signet programs.
- Differential expression matrices highlight subtype-restricted transcripts such as BEST4, SPIB, POU2AF1, PTPRK, CEACAM5/6, TACSTD2, CXCL1/5/6, CLDN3/4, AGR2, REG4, and ECM remodelers (COL11A1, COL12A1, LOXL2).
- Justifies expanding tumor subtypes with additional mucins (MUC1/3A/3B/5C/7/12/15/17/21/22), neuroendocrine and synaptic genes (UNC13A/B, PCLO, CACNA1B/E, SCG5), and RAS/MAPK-responsive secretory factors (PIGR, WFDC2, SPINK7).

## 2. Smillie, C.S. et al. *Intra- and inter-cellular rewiring of the human colon during ulcerative colitis.* Cell 178, 714-730 (2019). GEO: GSE116222

- Single-cell RNA-seq of 365,000 epithelial, stromal, and immune cells from healthy colon plus ulcerative colitis patients, resolving normal absorptive colonocytes, goblet cells, BEST4+ ionocytes, tuft cells, reserve stem cells, Paneth-like cells, enteroendocrine subsets, and PI16+ fibroblasts.
- Provides high-confidence markers for secretory lineages (BEST2, SLC4A5, TMEM52B, POU2AF1, DCLK1, LRMP), innate lymphoid-like tuft effectors (IL17RB, IL25, ADGRG4, TRPM5, TAS2R3/46), and enteric glial transcriptional states (SLC7A10, KCNJ10, GJB2/6, LAMA1/B1/B2).
- Supports inclusion of mucosal barrier junction genes (CLDN15, EPCAM, ITGA6/ITGB4), ion transporters (SLC26A4, SLC9A3, ATP6V1B1/V0D2), and inflammatory alarmins (SAA1/2, DUOX1/A1, NOS2) for epithelial resilience tracking.

## 3. Lee, H.O. et al. *Lineage-dependent gene expression programs in colorectal cancer.* Nature Genetics 52, 594-603 (2020). GEO: GSE132465

- scRNA-seq across paired tumor and adjacent mucosa from multiple colorectal patients describing malignant stem/TA, enterocyte-like, goblet-like, tuft/BEST4+, enteroendocrine-like, mucinous, and neuroendocrine carcinoma clusters.
- Demonstrates coexisting secretory-hybrid states expressing MUC5B, TFF2, SPDEF, ELF3, KIAA1324, TMEM45B, and interferon-responsive REG4/IFI27 modules.
- Highlights neuroendocrine outliers enriched for CHGA/B, ASCL1, INSM1, DLL3, PEG10 along with synaptic vesicle machinery (VAMP2, STXBP1, SYT7/9, RAB3A/B), motivating their addition to tumor marker panels.

## 4. Kinchen, J. et al. *Structural remodeling of the human colonic mesenchyme in inflammatory bowel disease.* Cell 175, 372-386 (2018). ArrayExpress: E-MTAB-6738

- scRNA-seq of purified lamina propria stromal cells identifying discrete submucosal fibroblasts, perivascular fibroblasts, PI16+ adventitial fibroblasts, pericytes, vascular and lymphatic endothelial populations, Schwann cells, and serosal mesothelium.
- Reports subtype-restricted ECM and signaling genes such as COL8A1/A2, COL14A1, COL28A1, MFGE8, ANGPTL1/2/4/8, ADAMTS12/ADAMTSL2/4, HEY1/2, TEK, TIE1, NRP1/2, CCBE1, PROCR, ITGA7/8, ITGB3/5, PALLD, CD248, and PTX3.
- Validates the expanded stromal marker lists ensuring each fibroblast, endothelial, pericyte, Schwann, and mesothelial subtype exceeds the 50-marker target while preserving biologic specificity.

## 5. GTEx Consortium. *The GTEx Consortium atlas of genetic regulatory effects across human tissues.* Science 369, 1318-1330 (2020). dbGaP: phs000424.v9 (colon transverse and sigmoid)

- Bulk RNA-seq from healthy colon segments used to confirm baseline expression for absorptive enterocytes (SLC26A3, CA2, ALPI, FABP1), smooth muscle (ACTA2, TAGLN, MYH11, CNN1, MYLK), pericytes (RGS5, CSPG4, MCAM), and vascular endothelium (PECAM1, ESAM, KDR, ROBO4).
- Provides background levels for collagen-rich fibroblasts (COL1A1/2, COL3A1, COL5A1/2, COL6A1/2/3, LOX/LOXL1), complement regulators (VTN, CLU), and mucosal immune chemokines (CCL21, CXCL12, CXCL14) incorporated into normal cell-type marker sets.

## 6. Uhlén, M. et al. *The Human Protein Atlas in 2023.* Nucleic Acids Research 51, D1057-D1073 (2023). Available at <https://www.proteinatlas.org/> (tissue: colon)

- Protein-level validation via IHC for epithelial mucins (MUC1/2/3A/3B/12/13/15/17/21/22), transcription factors (SPDEF, ELF3, POU2F3), tuft markers (DCLK1, AVIL, LRMP), Paneth-like antimicrobials (LYZ, DEFA5, DEFA6, REG4), and Schwann/peripheral nerve components (MPZ, PRX, NCAM1/2).
- Confirms localization of vascular and lymphatic markers (PECAM1, TEK, PROX1, FOXC2, FLT4, GJA4/5), mesothelial keratins (KRT8/18/19), and ECM proteins (VCAN, COL15A1, COL18A1) updated in the colorectal tab.
