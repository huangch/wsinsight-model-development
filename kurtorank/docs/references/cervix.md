# Cervix References

## 1. The Cancer Genome Atlas Research Network. *Integrated genomic and molecular characterization of cervical cancer.* Nature 543, 378-384 (2017). doi:10.1038/nature21386

- Bulk RNA-seq, exome, copy-number, and methylation profiles from 307 TCGA-CESC tumors (squamous, adenocarcinoma, and small-cell neuroendocrine).
- Differential expression tables and subtype annotations highlight keratinization programs (TP63, KRT5/6/14/16/17, SERPINB3/4), interferon signaling (STAT1/2, CXCL9/10/11), mucinous secretory pathways (MUC1/4/5B/16, CEACAM5/6, AGR2/3, TFF1/2/3), and neuroendocrine modules (CHGA/B, INSM1, DLL3, PEG10, ASCL1).
- Justifies expansion of malignant panels with additional squamous-basal ECM remodelers (LAMC2, LAMB3, ITGB8, FSCN1, SERPINB2/B8/B11, COL7A1, PXDN), adenocarcinoma mucins (MUC12/15/21/22, CFTR, CLDN23), and neuroendocrine synaptic genes (UNC13A/B, PCLO, CACNA1B/E, RAB3A/B).

## 2. He, J. et al. *Single-cell RNA sequencing reveals the tumor ecosystem of HPV-associated cervical cancer.* Nature Communications 12, 5633 (2021). GEO: GSE168652

- scRNA-seq of >40,000 cells spanning malignant squamous/adenocarcinoma programs, neuroendocrine-like states, stromal fibroblasts, perivascular cells, endothelial cells, and multiple immune lineages.
- Reported keratinocyte transition states at the tumor-stroma interface (KRTDAP, KPRP, SBSN, SPRR3/4), collagen-remodeling CAF signatures (COL11A2, LAMA4, ADAMTSL2/4, LOXL4), contractile smooth-muscle/pericyte genes (SYNPO2, LDB3, ITGA1/4/9, ITGB3/6, PDE5A, PTGIS), and angiogenic endothelial cues (ACKR1, COL4A1/2, ANGPTL1/2, BMP4/6, MFSD2A, ESM1, GJA4/5).
- Also delineated reserve basal/SCJ progenitors expressing NOTCH/JAG ligands, LRIG1, BMI1, ID1/3, FGFR2/3, and transitional secretory programs enriched for REG4, CXCL17, LEFTY1/2, LIF/LIFR, and WFDC-family genes.

## 3. Qi, H. et al. *Single-cell transcriptomic atlas of the healthy human cervix and transformation zone.* Cell Reports 35, 109303 (2021). GEO: GSE151666

- Profiled ectocervical squamous layers, reserve basal cells, squamocolumnar junction (SCJ) cells, endocervical secretory cells, and resident stromal/vascular compartments in HPV-negative donors.
- Identified barrier-enriched suprabasal clusters with IL36A/G, CXCL14/17, CALML5, SPINK5/9, and late cornified envelope genes (LCE1C/1E/3E/3F); basal stem pools with LRIG1, HOPX, ITGA4/9/10, LAMA5, NOTCH4, DLL1/4, JAG1/2; and SCJ transitional programs co-expressing CFTR, CLDN23, TACSTD1, PAX8, IL1RL1, TNFAIP6, and WFDC1/2.
- Provides baseline expression for fibroblasts (CRTAC1, FNDC1, PCOLCE/PCOLCE2) and endothelial subsets (SPNS2, CALCRL, PRKG1) incorporated into the updated markers.

## 4. GTEx Consortium. *The GTEx Consortium atlas of genetic regulatory effects across human tissues.* Science 369, 1318-1330 (2020). dbGaP: phs000424.v9 (cervix uteri tissue)

- Bulk RNA-seq from healthy cervix uteri provides comparative expression for smooth muscle (ACTA2, TAGLN, DES, CAV1/2, MYLK isoforms), fibroblasts (COL1A1/2, COL3A1, MFAP2/4/5), and endothelial cells (PECAM1, TEK, PROCR, S1PR1, ROBO4).
- Used to confirm inclusion of housekeeping transporters (SLC34A2/A3, SLC26A3/A4, CFTR) and stromal ECM genes (COL18A1, COL21A1, COL22A1) within normal cervix subtypes.

## 5. Uhlén, M. et al. *The Human Protein Atlas in 2023.* Nucleic Acids Research 51, D1057-D1073 (2023). Available at <https://www.proteinatlas.org/> (tissue: cervix, endocervix, ectocervix)

- Immunohistochemistry plus transcript estimates validating protein-level localization for keratins (KRT5/14, KRT7/8/18/19/20), mucins (MUC1/4/16/20), WFDC/SLPI family, stromal collagens, and endothelial adhesion molecules.
- Supports assignment of new markers such as SPINK7, WFDC8, BPIFB1/BPIFA1, REG4, ANGPTL2, and CXCL17 to the respective epithelial or vascular segments.
