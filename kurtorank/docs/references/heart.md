# Heart References

## 1. Behjati, S. et al. *Genomic and transcriptomic analyses reveal driver events in primary angiosarcoma.* Nature Genetics 46, 376-379 (2014)

- Sequenced human angiosarcoma cohorts showing recurrent KDR, PTPRB, PLCG1, and VEGFA axis activation plus ETS/ERK transcriptional amplifications (FLI1, ETS1, ETS2) and angiogenic adhesion genes (PECAM1, CLDN5, ROBO4, PROCR).
- Supports inclusion of extra endothelial-tumor markers (SOX7/18, KLF2/4, ANGPTL2, COL18A1) and inflammatory cytokines (CXCL8, IL6) within the cardiac angiosarcoma subtype.

## 2. Shern, J.F. et al. *Comprehensive genomic analysis of rhabdomyosarcoma reveals distinct genetic subtypes.* Nature Genetics 46, 129-135 (2014)

- Defines the fusion-positive (PAX3/7–FOXO1) versus fusion-negative landscape with strong expression of myogenic regulators (MYOD1, MYOG, MYF5, MEF2C/D, HAND2) and oncogenic kinases (FGFR4, ALK, MET, IGF1R).
- Provides justification for expanding rhabdomyosarcoma markers with paired developmental transcription factors (SIX1/2, TBX2/3, NKX2-5) and pro-migratory receptors (CXCR4, STAT3, IL6) reflected in the CSV update.

## 3. Tabula Sapiens Consortium. *The Tabula Sapiens: A multiple-organ single-cell transcriptomic atlas of the human body.* Science 376, eabl4896 (2022). GEO: GSE194122

- Adult donor atlas capturing ventricular, atrial, and conduction cardiomyocytes (HCN1/2/4, CACNA1D/G, SCN5A/SCN10A, TBX3/5/18), stromal fibroblasts (COL12A1/14A1/16A1, PDGFRA/B, POSTN), pericytes (PDGFRB/L, RGS5, CSPG4, ITGA7/ITGB3), and macrophage subsets (LYVE1, MRC1, VSIG4, GPNMB).
- Validates the subtype-specific channel, junction, and ECM genes newly appended to the heart rows of `markers.csv`.

## 4. Chaffin, M. et al. *Single-nucleus profiling of human hearts identifies cell-type-specific remodeling in cardiomyopathy.* Nature 588, 466-472 (2020). GEO: GSE183852

- Single-nucleus RNA-seq across failing and non-failing human hearts highlighting ventricular versus atrial working myocytes (LMNA, BAG3, DSP/DSG2/DSC2, PKP4), sinus node pacemakers (HCN3, CACNA1I, GJD2, BMP2/4, ID2/3), and activated myofibroblasts (ITGA8, TAGLN2, SERPINH1, GREM1).
- Informs the extended conduction-system ion channel repertoire and contractile/ECM programs used to bring atrial, ventricular, pacemaker, and fibroblast subtypes above the 50-marker goal.

## 5. GTEx Consortium. *The GTEx Consortium atlas of genetic regulatory effects across human tissues.* Science 369, 1318-1330 (2020). dbGaP: phs000424.v9 (heart left ventricle and atrial appendage)

- Bulk RNA-seq confirming baseline abundance for sarcomeric genes (TTN, MYH6/7, MYBPC3, MYL2/3/4/7), calcium-handling components (RYR2, CASQ2, TRDN, FKBP1B, SLC8A1), endothelial/mesenchymal transcripts (KDR, TEK, COL1A1/1A2, COL3A1, LOX/LOXL1/2), and resident immune factors (CCL2, CCR2, CX3CR1, APOE, LPL).
- Used to cross-check subtype expansions for both cardiomyocyte and stromal compartments.

## 6. Uhlén, M. et al. *The Human Protein Atlas in 2023.* Nucleic Acids Research 51, D1057-D1073 (2023). Available at <https://www.proteinatlas.org/> (tissue: heart, left ventricle, atrium)

- Protein-level localization for sarcomeric complexes (DES, MYH6/7, MYL2/7, TNNT2), conduction-associated channels (HCN4, SCN5A, CACNA1D), vascular adhesion molecules (PECAM1, ICAM2, PROCR), and stromal ECM proteins (POSTN, VCAN, COL12A1/16A1, MATN2).
- Supports inclusion of the newly added ion-channel, junctional, and ECM targets across the heart subtypes.
