# Ovary References

## 1. Fan, X. et al. *Single-cell reconstruction of follicular remodeling in the human adult ovary.* Cell 177, 1177-1191 (2019)

- Resolves granulosa, theca interna, cumulus, luteinized granulosa, and stromal subtypes with coordinated steroidogenic, ECM, and cytokine programs (STAR/CYP11A1/HSD3B, FOXL2/FSHR/AMH, HAS2/PTX3/VCAN, INSL3/LHCGR/NR4A, LOX/THBS/PDGF axes) used to expand the corresponding normal ovary rows to ≥50 markers.

## 2. Zhang, Y. et al. *A single-cell atlas of human ovary identifies germline and somatic niche populations.* Nature Communications 12, 5255 (2021)

- Provides high-resolution transcriptomes for primordial oocytes (DDX4/DAZL/FIGLA/MEIOSIN/SYCP genes), ovarian hilum stem-like compartments (ALDH1A1/LGR5/L1CAM/YAP1), and cortico-medullary stromal fibroblasts (COL1A1/LUM/PDGFRA/TGFB1/CXCL12) underpinning the new germ-cell and stromal rows plus the enlarged stem-like signature.

## 3. The Cancer Genome Atlas Research Network. *Integrated genomic analyses of ovarian carcinoma.* Nature 474, 609-615 (2011)

- Defines the high-grade serous ovarian carcinoma landscape (ubiquitous TP53 loss, BRCA1/2/PARP pathway deficiencies, CCNE1 amplification, FOXM1-E2F mitotic programs, cytokine/JAK–STAT signaling) that informed the expanded HGSOC marker list with HRR, checkpoint, and immune-escape genes.

## 4. Tan, D.S.P. et al. *Genomic analyses of ovarian clear cell carcinoma reveal metabolic and angiogenic dependencies.* Nature Communications 10, 2195 (2019)

- Highlights HNF1B/HNF4A-driven metabolisms, ARID1A/PIK3CA alterations, oxidative stress pathways (SLC7A11, HK2, ENO1), and VEGF/KDR/FLT4 angiogenic signaling leveraged to extend the clear-cell carcinoma markers.

## 5. Anglesio, M.S. et al. *Molecular characterization of low-grade serous, endometrioid, and borderline ovarian tumors.* Nature Communications 8, 15299 (2017)

- Documents MAPK pathway mutations (KRAS/NRAS/BRAF/MAP2K1), ERBB/FGFR cooperation, WNT/β-catenin activation (CTNNB1, RSPO/FRIZZLED modules), and EMT/adhesion factors that shaped the expanded low-grade serous and endometrioid carcinoma entries.

## 6. Ryland, G.L. et al. *Comprehensive genomic profiling of mucinous ovarian carcinoma.* Nature Communications 6, 6984 (2015)

- Reports KRAS/GNAS/ERBB2 lesions, intestinal transcription factors (CDX2, HNF4A, SOX9), mucin/secretory programs (MUC2/5AC/17, REG family, AGR2/3/4), and chemokine expression (CXCL1-8) integrated into the mucinous carcinoma row.

## 7. Shah, S.P. et al. *Mutation of FOXL2 in granulosa-cell tumors of the ovary.* New England Journal of Medicine 360, 2719-2729 (2009)

- Establishes FOXL2 C134W-driven transcription, TGFβ/BMP receptor signaling, ECM remodeling, and angiogenic modules in adult granulosa tumors and thecoma/theca-derived lesions, guiding additions to both the AGCT and thecoma marker sets.

## 8. Ramos, P. et al. *SMARCA4 inactivation defines a unique ovarian small cell carcinoma hypercalcemic type.* Nature Genetics 46, 438-443 (2014)

- Characterizes SMARCA4-deficient SCCOHT with pluripotency (LIN28A/B, HMGA2), neuroendocrine (ASCL1, INSM1), calcium-handling (PTHLH, CYP24A1, TRPV6), and receptor tyrosine kinase signaling (KIT/PDGFRA/FGFR2) motifs incorporated into the SCCOHT row.

## 9. Zheng, H. et al. *Integrated multi-omic profiling of ovarian carcinosarcoma identifies mesenchymal and immune vulnerabilities.* Clinical Cancer Research 28, 214-228 (2022)

- Describes dual epithelial–mesenchymal programs (KRT8/18 with COL11A1/POSTN/ITGA11), TGFβ/JAK–STAT activation, and chemokine secretion (CCL2/CCL5/CXCL10) captured in the carcinosarcoma expansion.
