# Lung References

## 1. Travaglini, A.J. et al. *A molecular cell atlas of the human lung from single-cell RNA sequencing.* Nature 587, 619-625 (2020)

- Defines transcriptional programs for AT1 (AGER, PDPN, HOPX, RTKN2, MFSD2A/CAV1), AT2 (SFTPC, SFTPA/B/D, ABCA3, NAPSA, LPCAT1), club, goblet, ciliated, basal, and ionocyte lineages with zonation-aware integrin/claudin repertoires reflected in the expanded epithelial rows.

## 2. Reyfman, P.A. et al. *Single-cell transcriptomic analysis of human lung provides insights into the pathobiology of pulmonary fibrosis.* American Journal of Respiratory and Critical Care Medicine 199, 1517-1536 (2019)

- Resolves adventitial fibroblasts, lipofibroblasts, pericyte/SMC states, alveolar macrophages, and endothelial subtypes with chemokine, scavenger receptor, and lipid-metabolism signatures (e.g., CX3CR1, MERTK, ABCA1/G1, ANGPT2) that were incorporated into the connective and immune blocks.

## 3. Plasschaert, L.W. et al. *A single-cell atlas of the airway epithelium reveals the CFTR-rich pulmonary ionocyte.* Nature 560, 377-381 (2018)

- Introduces the FOXI1⁺/CFTR⁺ pulmonary ionocyte lineage and catalogs club, goblet, basal, and ciliated differentiation trajectories with characteristic v-ATPase subunits, transporter, and secretory gene sets used to extend those rows to ≥50 markers.

## 4. Tabula Sapiens Consortium. *The Tabula Sapiens: A multiple-organ single-cell transcriptomic atlas of the human body.* Science 376, eabl4896 (2022)

- Confirms pulmonary capillary versus lymphatic endothelial programs (KDR/TEK vs PROX1/LYVE1/FLT4), stromal heterogeneity, and resident immune states, validating the added adhesion, Notch, and extracellular matrix markers for endothelial and fibroblast lineages.

## 5. The Cancer Genome Atlas Research Network. *Comprehensive molecular profiling of lung adenocarcinoma.* Nature 511, 543-550 (2014)

- Provides oncogenic drivers (EGFR, ERBB2/3/4, KRAS, BRAF, MET, ALK, ROS1, RET) plus downstream PI3K/AKT/YAP signaling, EMT mediators, and immune checkpoint modules used to broaden the lung adenocarcinoma tumor row.

## 6. Campbell, J.D. et al. *Comprehensive genomic characterization of squamous cell lung cancers.* Nature 517, 576-582 (2015)

- Details TP63/SOX2-amplified programs, oxidative stress (NFE2L2/KEAP1), PI3K pathway lesions, FGFR amplifications, and keratin/serpin signatures that inform the expanded squamous carcinoma markers.

## 7. George, J. et al. *Comprehensive genomic profiles of small cell lung cancer.* Nature 524, 47-53 (2015)

- Catalogs neuroendocrine transcription factors (ASCL1, NEUROD1, INSM1), mitotic regulators (AURKA/B, PLK1, CDK1/2), and anti-apoptotic programs (BCL2, BIRC5) leveraged for the small-cell and large-cell neuroendocrine carcinoma rows.

## 8. Derks, J.L. et al. *New insights into the molecular characteristics of pulmonary carcinoids and large cell neuroendocrine carcinomas.* Nature Communications 9, 1042 (2018)

- Describes transcriptional regulators (FEV, OTP, LMX1A/B, HES6) and calcium/secretory machinery (SYT4/7, CACNA1A/C) distinguishing carcinoids from high-grade LCNEC, supporting the uncommon neuroendocrine subtype expansions.

## 9. Rekhtman, N. et al. *DNA sequencing of pulmonary sarcomatoid carcinoma reveals high tumor mutational burden and actionable MET pathway alterations.* Nature Communications 11, 4876 (2020)

- Highlights EMT, integrin, collagen-remodeling, cytokine, and MET/AXL/JAK activation programs used to elaborate the sarcomatoid marker list.

## 10. Yu, Y. et al. *Integrated genomic profiling of pulmonary salivary gland-type tumors.* Modern Pathology 34, 196-210 (2021)

- Characterizes MYB-NFIB/CRTC1-MAML2 fusions, NOTCH/KIT signaling, and mucin/keratin programs in pulmonary adenoid cystic and mucoepidermoid carcinomas, informing the rare glandular tumor rows.
