# Kidney References

## 1. The Cancer Genome Atlas Network. *Comprehensive molecular characterization of clear cell renal cell carcinoma.* Nature 499, 43-49 (2013)

- Describes hypoxia-driven transcriptional programs in TCGA-KIRC with VHL loss, HIF/EPAS1 stabilization, and metabolic rewiring (CA9, CA12, NDUFA4L2, SLC16A1, SLC7A11, HIGD1A) plus stromal cross-talk (ANGPTL4, CXCL12/CXCR4, VCAM1, ITGA5/ITGB1).
- Provides genomic evidence for recurrent chromatin remodeling mutations (BAP1, PBRM1, SETD2, KDM5C) that were appended to the clear cell carcinoma marker block.

## 2. The Cancer Genome Atlas Network. *Comprehensive molecular characterization of papillary renal-cell carcinoma.* New England Journal of Medicine 374, 135-145 (2016)

- Defines MET-driven type I versus NRF2/antioxidant type II papillary tumors, highlighting epithelial transporters (LRP2, CUBN, SLC5A12, SLC34A2, SLC13A3) and receptor tyrosine kinases (MET, EGFR, ERBB2/3/4) enriched in papillary lesions.
- Supports addition of PROM1, CD24, GATA3, ITGB5, and secretory glycoproteins (SUSD2, WFDC2) to the papillary carcinoma set.

## 3. The Cancer Genome Atlas Network. *Comprehensive molecular characterization of papillary renal cell carcinoma and chromophobe renal cell carcinoma.* Cell Reports 23, 313-326 (2018)

- Profiles chromophobe RCC with FOXI1/KIT signatures, vacuolar ATPase subunits (ATP6V1B1/C2, ATP6V1E1/F, ATP6V0C), intercalated cell channels (KCNJ10, KCNK5, CLDN4/8/18), and MiT/TFE pathway genes (MITF, TFEB, TFEC, KITLG).
- Provides rationale for expanding the chromophobe and collecting duct carcinoma entries with acid–base transporters, keratins (KRT14/17), cytokines (CXCL2/3/5/6), and invasion genes (SERPINE1, PLAUR, CD274).

## 4. Kidney Precision Medicine Project (KPMP) Consortium. *A multi-cohort atlas of the human kidney at single-cell resolution.* Science Advances 7, eabd3359 (2021)

- Integrates single-cell and single-nucleus RNA-seq across cortex and medulla to define podocytes (NPHS1/2, SYNPO, MAGI2, COL4A3-5, FAT1), mesangial cells (ITGA8, ITGA10, PDGFRA/B, COL6A1/2), glomerular/peritubular endothelial subsets (MFSD2A, EMCN, APLN, CLEC14A), and tubular segments (SLC5A11/12, SLC47A1/2, TRPV5/6, CLDN8/14).
- Directly underpins the added transporters, ECM modules, and signaling genes across normal kidney subtypes.

## 5. Lake, B.B. et al. *A single-nucleus RNA-sequencing pipeline to decipher the molecular anatomy of the human kidney.* Science 365, 1466-1473 (2019)

- Provides snRNA-seq delineation of proximal S1/S2, thick ascending limb, distal convoluted tubule, collecting duct principal/intercalated, juxtaglomerular renin cells, and resident macrophages.
- Highlights lineage-enriched ion channels (SLC12A2, KCNJ16, WNK3, KCNN4), aquaporins (AQP2/3/4/5/6), proton pumps (ATP6AP2, ATP6V1G3), and immune receptors (VSIG4, SIGLEC10, GPNMB) that were incorporated into the kidney section.

## 6. GTEx Consortium. *The GTEx Consortium atlas of genetic regulatory effects across human tissues.* Science 369, 1318-1330 (2020). dbGaP: phs000424.v9 (kidney cortex and medulla)

- Bulk RNA-seq benchmarks for nephron transporters (SLC34A1, SLC22A11/12, SLC17A2/3), tubular ion channels (TRPM6, TRPV5/6, KCNJ1/16, KCNK3/5), stromal ECM genes (COL1/3/4/6/8, LOX, LAMB1/2), endothelial regulators (KLF2/4/6/9, DLL4/JAG1, PROCR), and macrophage mediators (MARCO, VSIG4, TNFSF13B).
- Used to validate expression of the newly added transporter, channel, ECM, and immune genes across kidney subtypes.

## 7. Uhlén, M. et al. *The Human Protein Atlas in 2023.* Nucleic Acids Research 51, D1057-D1073 (2023). Available at <https://www.proteinatlas.org/> (tissue: kidney cortex, kidney medulla)

- Immunohistochemistry confirms localization of podocyte (WT1, SYNPO, COL4A3-5), proximal tubule (LRP2, DAB2, PDZK1), TAL/DCT (UMOD, SLC12A1, SLC12A3, CLDN16/19), collecting duct (AQP2/3/4, SCNN1A/B/G), and intercalated cell markers (FOXI1, ATP6V1B1, SLC4A1/4A9) as well as macrophage proteins (CD163, VSIG4).
- Supports the protein-level relevance of the kidney marker expansions made in `markers.csv`.
